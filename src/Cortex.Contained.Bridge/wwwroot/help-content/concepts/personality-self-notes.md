# Personality & Self-Notes

Two related settings shape how the assistant behaves, and they serve different
purposes. Both live under **Tenant Settings**.

## Personality

The **personality** (persona) defines who the assistant is: its name, tone, and
behavioral instructions — for example, "You are a concise, direct personal
assistant." Edit it under **Tenant Settings → Personality**.

- Changes take effect on the **next message**.
- The assistant can **modify its own personality** when you ask it to.
- A **Reset to Default** button restores the original persona.

## Self-Notes

**Self-notes** are the assistant's stable **operating principles** — how it works,
not who it is. They should change rarely.

- Use self-notes for durable working rules.
- Do **not** put user preferences or specific procedures here — those belong in
  [long-term memory](help:memory-system) or skills, which are meant to grow and
  change.
- Like personality, self-notes have **Save** and **Reset to Default** controls.

## Which goes where?

| Kind of information | Where it belongs |
|---------------------|------------------|
| Who the assistant is (name, tone, style) | Personality |
| How the assistant operates (stable principles) | Self-notes |
| Facts about you, your preferences, your projects | Memory |
| Specific repeatable procedures | Memory / skills |

Keeping these separate is what lets personality and principles stay stable while
memory accumulates the ever-changing details.
